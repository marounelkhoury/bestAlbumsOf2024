The Hard Quartet

The Hard Quartet is the debut studio album by American supergroup The Hard Quartet, released on October 4, 2024, through Metador Records. It is their first full-length studio album release since 2021's Happier Than Ever. The Hard Quartet co-wrote The Hard Quartet with their members and frequent collaborators Stephen Malkmus, Matt Sweeney, Jim White, and Emmett Kelly, who also produced the album. Stylistically, it has been labeled as an alternative album. Upon release, The Hard Quartet received universal acclaim from critics, praising the production, songwriting, and vocal performances. It topped the charts in over 20 countries, including Australia, Canada, Germany, Ireland, New Zealand, and the United Kingdom. In the United States, The Hard Quartet debuted at number two on the Billboard 200, and charted all 10 of its songs in the top 40 of the Billboard Hot 100. The track "Earth Hater" was released as the lead single concurrently. "Rio's Song" was released as the second single from the album to great commercial success, peaking at number two on Billboard Hot 100 and in the top ten of several charts. At the 67th Annual Grammy Awards, the album and its songs received a total of seven nominations, including Album of the Year, Best Alternative Album, Song of the Year and Record of the Year. To support the album, The Hard Quartet has embarked on their seventh concert tour, The Hard QuartetThe Tour, which commenced on September 29, 2024.

Background

The Hard Quartet co-wrote and recorded their debut studio album, Happier Than Ever, at their members' home recording studio, located in the basement of Stephen Malkmus' Los Angeles residence. Recording took place weekly between April 2023 and February 2024, with the track listing finalized by the quartet during the album's creation. Released on October 4, 2024, The Hard Quartet was met with critical acclaim and commercial success, topping the Billboard 200 and nominated twice at the 67th Annual Grammy Awards. The Hard Quartet surprise-released their second extended play (EP) Guitar Songs in July 2025. The following year, they released "What Was I Made For?" for the soundtrack to the fantasy-comedy film Barbie (2023); they were inspired to write the song after seeing unfinished scenes of the film during its production. The song won an Academy Award for Best Original Song and Grammy Awards for Song of the Year and Best Song Written for Visual Media. In December 2023, The Hard Quartet began formulating ideas for their debut studio album with their members. In an interview with Zane Lowe for Apple Music, they mentioned that they hoped to start writing the album in 2024. In December 2024, The Hard Quartet said on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon that the album was "almost done". Three months later, they confirmed that the album had been mastered. The album art, which was shot by underwater photographer William Drumm, depicts The Hard Quartet falling through a door frame into dark blue water. The Hard Quartet described the photoshoot, which took six hours to complete and involved them being repeatedly submerged for two minutes at a time with a weight attached to their shoulders and without goggles or nose plugs, as being one of the most painful experiences of their life.
